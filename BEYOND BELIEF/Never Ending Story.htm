Subject: Never Ending Story
Looking back, I'd have to say that overall, I had a good childhood.  Of course there are things that I think about and wonder had they never happened, what would life be like now?  But I'm quickly assured by my belief that everything happens for a reason, and that every experience or person in my life was there to teach me a valuable lesson or plant a seed that would grow many years later.
 
When I think of my childhood, my mind is filled with so many memories, both good and bad, but nevertheless, memories.  One of the hardest things about writing this book was choosing which memories and stories to share with you.  If you stop to think about it, the mind is one powerful piece of machinery, storing so much information for a lifetime, filing and grouping bits of information and memories.  I realized early in life that I had a niche for memorizing numbers.  I can remember what our next-door-neighbors phone number was back when I was a little kid.  Or our one neighbor, who we'd hear on our "four-party phone line" talking to her lover about how much she wished she could be with him.  Those were good times. 
 
My neighbors played a big part of forming who I am today.  My second mom, Keitha (as I liked to call her), taught me sarcasm and patience.  God certainly blessed that woman with the patience of Job to handle all that she's handled over the years.  About a year ago, she popped in my head and a memory came to me about this song she would sing when we'd be in her Subaru going somewhere,  "Don't mess with my toot-toot."  I had to send her a copy of it, so I downloaded it online and sent it to her.  I spent a lot of my childhood at her house and her relatives, who were also neighbors down our road.  They called our road "Holliday holler," because almost everyone who lived down that road beared the last name Holliday.  My family was of a different branch.  They were all Catholics and we were Baptists.  I never was successful at trying to draw a family tree.  My grandfather passed away, and he seemed to be the only link to any knowledge of our family's past.
